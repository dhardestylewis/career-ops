## Most Serious Risks
*What do you see as the most serious risks at the intersection of AI and biology, and why? (Max 350 words)*

**Draft (196 words):**
> The most serious risk is that the democratization of dual-use AI bio-design tools fundamentally shifts the bottleneck of engineered pandemics from "knowledge acquisition" to "synthesis and detection." Historically, engineering a pathogen required tacit wet-lab knowledge, but as frontier models automate bio-design, the threat landscape shifts from a few highly monitored state actors to a massively decentralized network of adversaries. The catastrophic vulnerability is that our current defensive infrastructure relies entirely on pathogen-specific surveillance, which cannot detect a novel "stealth" pathogen engineered for high transmission and delayed lethality until geometric spread has already occurred. This presents an asymmetric attack surface on our physical urban infrastructure, exacerbated by a complete lack of real-time, pathogen-agnostic environmental monitoring. To mitigate this unmitigable existential threat, we must scale our macro-spatial biosurveillance compute infrastructure—transitioning from reactive medical countermeasures to proactive, continuous spatial sequencing—faster than AI scales biological capabilities.
