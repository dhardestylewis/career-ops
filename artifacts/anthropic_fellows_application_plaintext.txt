Why are you interested in participating in the Fellows program?
*
The frontier of AI capabilities is increasingly intersecting with global economic systems, where the deployment of advanced models will have massive societal impacts. I am applying to the Fellows program because I want to help build the quantitative infrastructure to evaluate and align these models with human economic prosperity. My background bridges elite academic research in complex systems with end-to-end commercial execution in economic forecasting. I previously led the primary ML infrastructure project for a $40M DARPA disaster resiliency initiative at TACC, working directly with leading scientists to model the societal and physical impacts of climate events at a national scale. From there, I founded Homecastr to model macroeconomic phenomena, building an automated valuation model that consistently outperforms corporate baselines like Zillow's nationwide housing forecasts.

I am drawn to Anthropic because of its unparalleled commitment to understanding the societal impacts of frontier AI. Building high-stakes physical models at TACC taught me that intelligent systems without reliable guardrails can be dangerous. The Fellows program represents the ideal environment to bridge my background in large-scale ML systems with the absolute frontier of AI policy. By collaborating directly with the Economics & Policy team, I want to apply my supercomputing foundation and startup execution to model, forecast, and shape the macroeconomic impacts of Anthropic's most advanced systems.

With your selected team(s) in mind, tell us briefly about one or more research areas you're excited about right now, and why.
*
I am particularly excited about the challenge of forecasting the macroeconomic and societal impacts of frontier AI deployment, specifically how agentic systems will interface with complex physical and economic environments like housing markets. While building the predictive architecture for Homecastr and scaling climate models for the state of Texas, I learned firsthand how difficult it is to ensure model reliability under distribution shift and adversarial conditions. I view the gap between theoretical economic modeling and the reality of AI-driven market disruption as a critical policy hurdle. I am fascinated by the prospect of using Anthropic's models to simulate vast, multi-agent economic environments to test policy interventions before deployment, helping to build evaluation frameworks that ensure AI accelerates human prosperity without destabilizing critical societal infrastructure.
